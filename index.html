Short Bio

Dr Barros is an Assistant Professor (Lecturer) in the School of Computer Science and Electronic Engineering at the University of Essex, UK. He leads the Unconventional Communications and Computing Laboratory.

He earned his PhD in Computer Science from the Walton Institute, South East Technological University, Ireland, in 2016. He has held prestigious academic positions at Tampere University, Finland (MSCA-IF), and Waterford Institute of Technology, Ireland (IRC GOI Postdoc).

Dr. Barros has secured over €1.5M in research funding from the European Commission, BBSRC, Innovate UK, and others. He is a Senior Member of IEEE, a member of EPSRC and BBSRC Engineering Biology Peer Review Colleges, and a Fellow of the Higher Education Academy. He received the CONNECT Prof. Tom Brazil Excellence in Research Award in 2020.

With over 90 peer-reviewed publications in top journals and conferences, his research focuses on Biocomputing, Molecular Communication, Neuroengineering, AI in Biology, Digital Twins. He is an associate editor for IEEE Transactions on Molecular, Biological and Multi-Scale Communications and a review editor for Frontiers in Communications and Networks. He has also served as a guest editor for Frontier in Cellular Neuroscience and IEEE Transactions on Molecular, Biological and Multi-Scale Communications.

Research Interests

Molecular Intercellular Communications

I seek to design communication systems for synthetic and natural biological cells or bionano machines using the following information carriers: calcium signalling, spikes, synapes and quorum sensing.
Biocomputing

I seek to model and apply principles of synthetic biology and control theory to the design of biological bionano computing devices that can be used as fully biocompatible implantable sensors and actuators.
Implantable Biocyber Interfaces

I seek to design implantable biocyber interfaces, such as biochips, to perform fully biocompatible distributed sensing/actuation in living systems.
AI in Biology and Medicine

I seek to develop AI models for computational biology and microscopic images towards realistic digital reconstructions of human tissues under natural and disease conditions.
