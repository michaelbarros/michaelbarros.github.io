Short Bio

Dr Barros is an Assistant Professor (Lecturer) since June 2020 in the School of Computer Science and Electronic Engineering at the University of Essex, UK. He is also a MSCA-IF Research Fellow (part-time) at the Tampere University, Finland. He received the PhD in Computer Science at the South East Technological University*, Ireland, in 2016. He previously held multiple academic positions as a Research Fellow in the Waterford Institute of Technology, Ireland. He is a member of the Communications and Networks Research Group and the Computational Biophysics and Imaging Group.

He has over 70 research peer-reviewed scientific publications in top journals and conferences such as Nature Scientific Reports, IEEE Transactions on Communications, IEEE Transactions on Vehicular Technology, in the areas of molecular and unconventional communications, biomedical engineering, bionano science and Beyond 5G. Since 2020, he is a review editor for the Frontiers in Communications and Networks journal in the area of unconventional communications. He also served as guest editor for the IEEE Transactions on Molecular, Biological and Multi-Scale Communications and Digital Communications Networks journals.

He received the CONNECT Prof. Tom Brazil Excellence in Research Award in 2020. Dr Barros is awarded the European Commission's Marie Skłodowska-Curie Individual Fellowship (MSCA-IF) from 2019-2021, the Irish Research Council's (IRC) Government of Ireland Post-doc Fellow from 2016-2018 and the Enterprise Ireland's (EI) Commercialization Funding from 2018-2019, securing a total of €667k as a PI. Dr Barros is a member of the IEEE and a Fellow of the Higher Education Academy.

Research Interests

Molecular Intercellular Communications

I seek to design communication systems for synthetic and natural biological cells or bionano machines using the following information carriers: calcium signalling, spikes, synapes and quorum sensing.
Biocomputing

I seek to model and apply principles of synthetic biology and control theory to the design of biological bionano computing devices that can be used as fully biocompatible implantable sensors and actuators.
Implantable Biocyber Interfaces

I seek to design implantable biocyber interfaces, such as biochips, to perform fully biocompatible distributed sensing/actuation in living systems.
Computational medicine

I seek to develop computational mathematical models that describe the signalling pathways changes at cellular level when biological tissues undergo diseases, including Alzeihmer's and Epilepsy, or pharmacological treatment.
